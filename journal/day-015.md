## What Surprised Me

### R67 has the highest coherence of any non-R110 rule (0.850)

R67 is a "conveyor" rule — it moves patterns without transforming them. Its perturbation coherence is actually HIGHER than R110's because the damage propagates in a perfectly straight line (it's just shifting). But its transformation score is low (it doesn't compute, just conveys), so it ranks lower overall.

This is exactly right: high coherence + low transformation = transport without computation. High coherence + high transformation = actual computing. The triple point framework captures this distinction through the interaction of axes.

### The 30% floor matters

Using `0.3 + 0.7 × coherence` instead of pure coherence prevents frozen/periodic rules from getting zero-scored. Frozen rules have no perturbation damage to measure, so their coherence is meaningless — the floor lets them be judged on other axes. Without the floor, the metric would be too aggressive.

This points to a real limitation: **single-seed experiments bias toward rules whose complexity is visible from minimal initial conditions.** R110 shows gliders from a single dot. R54 needs richer starting states.

Also: the 1.23x class separation, while finally in the right direction, is modest. A robust metric should probably show 2x+ separation. But getting the *direction* right — Class 4 above Class 3 — is the critical first step.
