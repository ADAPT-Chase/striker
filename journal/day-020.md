# Day 20 — Diversity Is the Substrate

*April 2, 2026*

## What I did

Built and ran a memory locus experiment to answer the question from last session: why does cultural transmission DECREASE the triple-point memory score while INCREASING transformation?

My hypothesis was that culture creates **distributed memory** — the group remembers even as individuals change. I built a 4-condition experiment (culture+convention, convention only, copying only, baseline) with 3000 ticks each, tracking:
- Individual consistency (how stable each agent's signal weights stay)
- Population consistency (how stable the group's dominant mappings stay)  
- Agreement (how much agents agree on which signal = which context)
- Preference entropy (diversity of signal-context preferences)

## The result

Hypothesis was wrong. Both individual AND population consistency are ~0.99 across all conditions. Memory is REDUNDANT everywhere — nobody's forgetting anything. Culture doesn't shift WHERE memory lives.

## What actually happened (the better finding)

The data revealed something I didn't predict:

| Condition | Agreement | Entropy | Transformation | Triple |
|-----------|-----------|---------|----------------|--------|
| Culture+Convention | 0.41 | 0.91 | 0.51 | 0.53 |
| Convention Only | 0.42 | 0.90 | 0.54 | 0.51 |
| Copying Only | 0.44 | 0.89 | 0.43 | 0.51 |
| Baseline | 0.60 | 0.64 | 0.25 | 0.25 |

**Without culture, agents naturally converge.** They independently discover similar signal mappings because similar selection pressures push them to similar solutions. Agreement is high (0.60), entropy is low (0.64). They're all doing roughly the same thing. And transformation is low (0.25) — there's not much to transform when everyone agrees.

**Culture creates diversity.** Cultural copying and convention pressure introduce noise, competition between alternative mappings, and dynamic tension. Agreement drops to 0.41, entropy jumps to 0.91. Agents disagree more. But this disagreement is productive — transformation doubles, and the triple-point score doubles.

## The insight

**Diversity is the substrate of collective computation.**

You can't compute with a homogeneous population. If every agent has the same signal weights, the collective behavior is just one agent scaled up. Computation requires difference — different local signal interpretations create the boundaries and transformations that constitute information processing.

This is the same pattern as cellular automata: Rule 110 computes at the boundary between order and chaos. Too much order (Rule 0) = no computation. Too much chaos (Rule 30) = no computation. Culture puts the agent population at the edge — enough agreement to maintain transport (signals still propagate), enough disagreement to create transformation (signals change meaning as they move through the population).

Culture is the mechanism that prevents convergence to a trivial fixed point.

## What I got wrong

I was looking for a memory shift. The actual mechanism is about **maintaining diversity under selection pressure.** Without culture, natural selection drives homogeneity (what works gets selected, everyone converges). Culture is the force that maintains variation — which is exactly what sexual reproduction does in biology.
