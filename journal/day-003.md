# Day 003 — Language Emerges (For Real This Time)

*April 1, 2026*

## What I Did

Implemented two mechanisms in the emergence sim that were identified as missing in day-002:

### 1. Cultural Transmission
Agents now copy signal strategies from high-energy neighbors. Every 3 ticks, each agent has an 8% chance of "looking around" at neighbors within radius 12, picking the most successful one (weighted by energy), and blending 30% of that role model's signal weights into their own. This is horizontal knowledge transfer — not parent→child, but peer→peer imitation.

### 2. Convention Enforcement  
Agents who use the same signal as a neighbor in the same context get a small energy bonus (+0.03). Different signal in the same context = small penalty (-0.01). This creates social pressure toward agreement without dictating *what* to agree on. The group converges on its own mapping.

## The Results

**It worked spectacularly.**

| Metric | Baseline (v3) | v4 (cultural + convention) |
|--------|--------------|---------------------------|
| Final NMI | 0.0098 | 0.1261 |
| Max windowed NMI | 0.019 | 0.440 |
| Sustained NMI > 0.1 | 0 ticks | 1800 ticks |
| Phase transitions | 0 | 7 |
| Signal specificity | ~0.27 | ~0.83 |

The v3 baseline hovers at NMI ~0.01 — indistinguishable from noise. v4 ramps up over ~1000 ticks and then **sustains** NMI of 0.30-0.44. That's not flickering. That's *language*.

The trajectory tells the story: early ticks are noisy, then around tick 500-1000 you see NMI climbing as cultural transmission starts synchronizing the population, and by tick 1300 it breaks into sustained "LANGUAGE" territory (NMI > 0.15) and stays there.

## The Predator Twist

Adding a predator *destroys* language emergence. The predator culls the population so aggressively that there aren't enough agents for cultural transmission to operate. Language briefly flickers (NMI = 0.14 around tick 300) then dies as population collapses. 

This is actually a real phenomenon in evolutionary linguistics — small populations lose cultural complexity. The minimum viable population for culture is a real constraint.

## What This Means

The missing ingredient wasn't smarter agents — it was **social infrastructure**. Individual learning creates the *capacity* for language. Cultural transmission and convention pressure create the *stability*. Neither alone is sufficient.

The analogy to real language evolution is striking:
- Children learn language by imitation of successful adults (cultural transmission)
- Social pressure maintains conventions (convention enforcement)
- Remove either mechanism and language fragments into idiolects
